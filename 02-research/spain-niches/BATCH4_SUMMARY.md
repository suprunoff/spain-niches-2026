## Scoring Methodology

**Formula:**
```
Score = (D×20 + G×20 + R×15 + S×15 + M×10 + A×10 + F×5 + T×5) / 100
```

**Dimensions (1-10 scale):**
- **D (Demand):** Do customers want this? Market pain level, willingness to pay
- **G (Gap):** Competitive positioning. Is there a clear gap vs incumbents?
- **R (Retention):** Sticky product? Recurring revenue, switching costs, regulatory moat
- **S (Size):** TAM/SAM sizing. Is the market big enough?
- **M (Monetization):** Unit economics. Can you charge enough?
- **A (Achievability):** Can you build and scale this in 12 months?
- **F (Founder fit):** Domain knowledge required. How much pre-existing expertise needed?
- **T (Timeline):** MVP build speed (weeks, maps to MVP_scope)

### 2️⃣ Occupational Health SaaS (Prevención de Riesgos Laborales) — 7.4/10
**Why Now?**
- **Hard regulation:** 1.5M Spanish companies >6 employees MUST have PRL compliance (Law 31/95)
- Market: €60M addressable (SaaS penetration 5% of €1.2B traditional SPA market)
- Gap: No Spanish SaaS for SMBs; 95% use manual spreadsheets or expensive traditional SPA (€800-2K/year)
- Stickiness: Regulatory mandate = 2% annual churn (extremely sticky)

**First 100 Customers:**
1. Partnerships: 10 HR SaaS platforms (Factorial, BambooHR, Workable) → white-label
2. B2B: HR consultants + business associations (CEOE, CEPYME)
3. Content: Webinars on "PRL digital compliance" (free)
4. Direct: 50 mid-market companies via LinkedIn

**MVP (5 weeks):**
- Risk assessment questionnaire → risk matrix
- Document generator (PRL policies, training plans)
- Incident tracking + compliance checklist
- HR system API integration

**Validator:** CAC €100, LTV €2,400, Y1 ARR €800K (20K users @ €40/year)

### 3️⃣ Export Compliance SaaS (EUDR + Smallholder Farmers) — 7.2/10
**Why Now?**
- **Regulatory deadline:** EUDR compliance mandatory Jan 2027 (hard stop, can't delay)
- Market: €21M TAM (266K smallholder farms × €80/year SaaS)
- Gap: BatchLynx exists globally but weak Spain adoption; most farmers use manual spreadsheets
- Acquisition: Partner with farmer cooperatives (ASAJA, COAG) for bulk adoption

**First 100 Customers:**
1. Partnerships: 5-10 largest farmer cooperatives (handles 50K+ members)
2. Subsidized pilots: Agricultural associations fund first month
3. Content: "EUDR compliance made simple" webinars + guides
4. Target: Organic + export-oriented farmers (70% of 266K SMH)

**MVP (5 weeks):**
- Digital record-keeping (crop, pesticide, harvest)
- EUDR checklist + export doc generator
- Certificate management (organic, DOP labels)
- Mobile app for field data

**Validator:** CAC €80, LTV €960, Y1 ARR €100K (1,250 users). **Volume play** — requires acquisition scale.

### 4️⃣ Farm-to-Restaurant B2B Marketplace — 7.1/10
**Why Now?**
- **Trend:** Michelin chefs + high-end restaurants sourcing local/organic (sustainability)
- Market: €225M TAM (75K restaurants × €3K annual direct-sourced produce)
- Gap: Choco.com (EU-wide) established but weak Spain focus, no Bizum
- Demand: 70%+ high-end restaurants want to increase local sourcing

**First 100 Customers:**
1. Direct: 50 Michelin-starred + high-end restaurants (personalized outreach)
2. Farmer acquisition: ASAJA, COAG cooperative partnerships
3. Content: "Local sourcing" SEO + influencer chef partnerships
4. Moat: 0% commission month 1-3 (acquisition subsidy)

**MVP (6 weeks):**
- Farmer listing (produce, certifications, delivery zones)
- Restaurant browsing + bulk ordering
- Bizum + SEPA invoicing
- Review system

**Risks:**
- Logistics (cold chain, spoilage) = operational burden
- Farmer reliability variable (supply inconsistency)
- Choco.com competition

**Validator:** CAC €120, LTV €18K (3 years), Y1 ARR €180K (25 restaurants × 25 farms)
