## Executive Summary

Spain's e-learning market is projected to grow at **9.15% CAGR (2024-2033)**, reaching €14B+ by 2033. Current market size (2026) sits at ~€700M–€1.2B depending on segment definition. The Spanish creator economy remains **3-5 years behind** the US/CIS markets, creating asymmetric opportunities:

1. **Market Size:** €700M–€1.2B TAM (online courses + e-learning services), growing 8–21.8% annually
2. **Key Tailwind:** Hotmart dominates (26M users globally, strong Brazil/LATAM), but **no Spanish-native all-in-one platform** comparable to GetCourse exists in Spain
3. **Payment Reality:** Bizum is **mandatory for consumer products** (30.6M users, 38% conversion preference), yet platforms like Teachable/Kajabi lack native support
4. **Corporate Training Gap:** FUNDAE system processes €50M+/year in employee training credits, but 70%+ goes unused; no SaaS bridges FUNDAE credits + online courses
5. **Biggest Vulnerability:** Hotmart takes **9.9% + €0.50 per sale**, no Bizum support, poor Spanish UI/support = migration risk for Spanish creators seeking local alternatives

## 3. SEPE/FUNDAE Market Analysis

### What is SEPE/FUNDAE?

**SEPE** = Servicio Público de Empleo Estatal (Spanish Public Employment Service)
**FUNDAE** = Fundación Estatal para la Formación en el Empleo (State Foundation for Employment Training)

### How It Works

1. Every Spanish company pays ~0.6% of payroll → **FUNDAE credits** (bonificaciones)
2. Each year, credits **reset** (€50M+ annual credits) — unused = lost
3. Companies can use credits to fund **employee training** through accredited providers
4. Training can be **online** (bimodal = virtual + in-person mixed) since 2026
5. Employees get **official certificates** recognized by Spanish labor ministry

### Market Size & Opportunity

- **4.6M workers trained annually** (2019 baseline, likely grown to 5–6M by 2026)
- **€50M+ annual credits** available (estimated €45–55M/year)
- **70% underutilization:** Most companies don't know how to use credits
- **Average spend per company:** €3,000–€12,000/year (depending on size)

### Top FUNDAE-Funded Training Areas (2026)

1. **Digital Skills** (AI, cybersecurity, cloud) — 29 new specialties added Jan 2026
2. **Languages** (English, tech English) — 33,553 resources available
3. **Management & Leadership** — cross-functional skills
4. **Technical Skills** (coding, data, networking)
5. **Soft Skills** (communication, project management)

### Competitive Landscape (B2B FUNDAE)

| Provider | Model | Approach |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **La Salle-URL** | Traditional university | Certifications, corporate programs |
| **Global4idiomas** | Language-focused | English + business Spanish |
| **Preply (B2B)** | Language tutoring + FUNDAE | 1:1 + group sessions |
| **CEDEFOP/SEPE** | Direct public providers | Free courses, lower quality |
| **Psicotec** | HR consulting + training | FUNDAE credit management |
| **[NO PLAYER]** | SaaS bridge | **Platform to connect FUNDAE credits + online courses** ← GAP |

### The FUNDAE SaaS Gap

**Problem:** Companies have €50M+ credits but no easy way to:
- Find FUNDAE-approved online courses
- Match employee skills gaps to courses
- Track compliance + certifications
- Manage credit allocation by department

**Current Solution:** Manual (email, spreadsheets, consultants) = 70% waste

**Opportunity:** Build FUNDAE + LMS SaaS = connect training demand to course supply

## 5. Top 2 Niches: Deep Dive & Unit Economics

### Niche #1 Deep Dive: All-in-One Infobiz Platform (GetCourse ES)

#### Strategic Thesis

Hotmart (9.9% + €0.50/sale) dominates Spanish creators (8K–12K), but **no Spanish-native platform exists** with:
- Zero commission or flat-fee model
- Bizum native payment (mandatory for Spanish consumer SaaS)
- All-in-one: course builder + email drip + community + affiliate system
- Spanish UI/support/compliance

GetCourse's success in Russia (€50M+ ARR, 50K+ creators) is a **proof of concept**. Spain is 3–5 years behind, but following identical pattern: creator economy growth + demand for local SaaS alternative to US platforms.

#### Unit Economics (Target: 500 creators at Y1)

**Assumption: 8% commission on creator revenue** (lower than Hotmart's 9.9%, attracting migration)

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **CAC (Creator)** | €120 | ProductHunt Spain, Hotmart community threads, affiliate referrals, LinkedIn |
| **LTV (Creator)** | €680 | 3.5-year lifetime × €163/year revenue (8% commission on €2K course revenue × 65% take-rate after refunds) |
| **Gross Churn** | 18%/month | Typical for creator platforms; 55% of creators quit in Y1; accelerates to 25% by M18 |
| **Net Churn** | 12%/month (adjusted) | After viral loops + founder acquisition of top creators (stabilize churn) |
| **Payback Period** | 7–9 months | €120 CAC ÷ (€163/yr ÷ 12) = 8.8 months |
| **LTV/CAC Ratio** | 5.7x | Healthy for early-stage creator platform (target: >3x) |
| **Founding Network** | 50 creators | Personal outreach, Hotmart migration, YouTube creators in network |
| **Growth Target** | +60 net new creators/month by M12 | 50 initial → 50 + (60 × 11) = 710 creators (accounting for churn) |

**Year 1 Revenue Projection:**

| Month | Gross Creators | Revenue (€) | Notes |
|-------|---|---|---|
| M0 | 50 | €650 | Founding creators |
| M3 | 100 | €1,600 | +50 cohort, viral loop starting |
| M6 | 180 | €3,200 | +80 cohort, stronger referrals |
| M9 | 300 | €5,600 | +120 cohort, YouTube partnerships kickin |
| M12 | 480 | €8,200 | +180 cohort, Hotmart migration peak |
| **Y1 ARR** | **480** | **€96,000 avg, €480K total if 50% stay** | Conservative: accounts for churn |

**Adjusted Y1 ARR:** €240K–€480K (depending on churn/stickiness assumptions)

#### Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Hotmart retaliates** | Medium | High | 1) Differentiate on Bizum + community; 2) Focus on niche (business coaches vs. general); 3) Partner with affiliate networks |
| **Churn > 20%** | High | High | 1) Implement cohort engagement loops; 2) Build creator community (Discord, live calls); 3) Invest in creator success (education, templates) |
| **Payment processing issues** | Low | High | 1) Diversify gateways (Bizum + Stripe + Redsys); 2) Hire local payments expert; 3) SEPA transfers backup |
| **Spanish creators prefer Hotmart** | Medium | Medium | 1) Win on niche verticals (coaching vs. infobiz); 2) Partnerships with creator agencies; 3) Pricing incentives (temporary discount) |
| **Lack of network effects** | Low | Medium | 1) Build marketplace (buyer-seller matching); 2) Community features; 3) Affiliate network integration |

#### MVP Scope (8–10 weeks, 2–3 developers)

**Core Features:**
1. **Course Builder** (Notion-like editor, video hosting via Vimeo/Bunny CDN, quizzes, certificates)
2. **Email Drip Automation** (basic workflows: welcome, abandoned, upsell, re-engage)
3. **Bizum + Stripe Integration** (payment processing via MONEI or Adyen)
4. **Creator Dashboard** (revenue, students, analytics, withdrawal)
5. **Student Portal** (course access, progress tracking, certificate download)
6. **Affiliate System** (referral links, payout tracking)
7. **Support** (intercom chat, knowledge base in Spanish)

**NOT in MVP:** Community (Discord suffices), advanced analytics, app (web-first), white-label

#### GTM (First 500 Creators)

**Phase 1 (M1–M3): Founder-led recruiting**
- Target: 50 founding creators
- Method: Personal outreach to top Hotmart creators (YouTube, LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Incentive: 6-month commission discount (5% instead of 8%), equity in referral program
- Success metric: 50 active creators, €1K/month revenue

**Phase 2 (M3–M6): Content + Affiliate**
- Target: +80 creators (180 total)
- Method: Launch ProductHunt Spain, create YouTube tutorials (in Spanish), partner with 3–5 creator agencies, seeded Reddit threads
- Incentive: Affiliate referrals (€20 per referred creator)
- Success metric: 30%+ month-over-month growth, 50+ organic signups

**Phase 3 (M6–M12): Paid acquisition + Partnerships**
- Target: +200 creators (380 total, growing toward 500)
- Method: Google Ads (keywords: "plataforma cursos online España", "alternativa Hotmart"), Facebook Spain creator groups, partnerships with YouTube education channels
- Incentive: Hotmart affiliate migration program (€50 credit for first 3 months)
- Success metric: CAC ≤ €120, LTV/CAC ≥ 5x

#### Defensibility & Moat

1. **Network Effects:** More creators = better affiliate referrals; more referrals = bigger creator pool
2. **Data Moat:** Student behavior, course performance, conversion patterns → insights for new creators
3. **Switching Cost:** Creator data (student emails, revenue history) hard to export; re-uploading courses = friction
4. **Regulation Advantage:** Being Spanish = easier SEPE/FUNDAE integration, RGPD compliance built-in

### Niche #2 Deep Dive: Corporate L&D + FUNDAE SaaS

#### Strategic Thesis

Spain's FUNDAE system processes **€50M+/year** in training credits, yet **70% goes unused** due to:
- Lack of awareness (HR managers don't know system)
- Complexity (which courses qualify? FUNDAE approval process = 6–12 weeks)
- Fragmentation (credits buried in payroll systems, no easy discovery of courses)

**Opportunity:** Build B2B SaaS to:
1. **Aggregate** FUNDAE-approved courses (partner with 50–100 training providers)
2. **Manage credits** (dashboard showing available credits, department allocation, expiry dates)
3. **Match employees** to courses (skills gap analysis, recommendations)
4. **Track compliance** (certificates, attendance records, SEPE reporting)
5. **Integrate** with HRIS (Personio, Factorial, SAP SuccessFactors) via API

**VeriFactu deadline (Jan 2027)** creates urgency: companies must digitize training records.

## 6. Market Gaps vs. Competitors

### Gap #1: Payment Methods (Bizum + Local Banking)

**The Problem:** Hotmart, Teachable, Kajabi, Thinkific, Podia = **all lack Bizum**
- Bizum: 30.6M users, 38% prefer for online purchases, €67.7B volume in 2025 (+53% YoY)
- Current workaround: Stripe (generic), PayPal (expensive, currency conversion)
- Impact: 15–25% conversion loss on Spanish checkout for courses <€50

**Opportunity:** Build payment gateway specializing in Spanish payment methods:
- Bizum (instant, mobile-first, fee: 0.3–0.5%)
- Tarjeta (traditional debit/credit, 1.5–2.5% fee)
- SEPA transfers (B2B, slow)
- Partner: MONEI, Adyen, Redsys (integrate all 3 in one dashboard)

**Go-to-Market:** White-label payment widget for Hotmart-like platforms. **Systeme.io already won here** (0% commissions + Spanish support attracts SMBs).

---

### Gap #2: Spanish-Native All-in-One Platform

**The Problem:** Hotmart dominates, but slow Spanish UI updates, no Bizum, 9.9% + €0.50 commission
- Systeme.io is closest (0% commission, Spanish available), but lacks community features + limited integrations
- GetCourse conquered Russia (€50M+ ARR), yet isn't in Spain

**Opportunity:** Build Spanish-native infobiz platform (GetCourse ES) with:
- 0% commission (recoup via marketplace premium, affiliate tools)
- Bizum + Stripe native
- Spanish UI/support from day 1
- Community features (Facebook Groups integration, Slack, Discord)
- Affiliate system pre-built
- FUNDAE integration (corporate training module)

**Proof of Concept:** GetCourse's model in Russia shows profitability at 50K creators. Spain = 8K–12K creators → 20% penetration = €3M–€5M ARR.

---

### Gap #3: Corporate FUNDAE Integration (B2B)

**The Problem:** FUNDAE processes €50M+/year in credits; 70% unused. No SaaS bridges:
- Company has credits but doesn't know which courses qualify
- Employees don't know training options
- No easy way to track spending, compliance, ROI

**Opportunity:** Build FUNDAE + LMS SaaS:
- Dashboard: show available credits, expirations, usage
- Marketplace: curated FUNDAE-approved courses (partner with 50+ providers)
- Matching: skills gap → course recommendations
- Compliance: track attendance, certificates, SEPE reporting
- Integrations: Personio, Factorial, SAP SuccessFactors

**TAM:** €50M credits × 10% penetration (5,000 companies) × €2K CAGR = €10M+ TAM
**CAC:** €800 (LinkedIn Ads + webinars)
**LTV:** €8,400 (3.5 years × €200/mo avg)
**Profitability:** Breakeven at ~100 customers (M6–M9)

---

### Gap #4: Spanish Language + Cultural Localization

**The Problem:** Global platforms (Udemy, LinkedIn Learning) use English, generic examples
- Spanish audience: 92% check reviews before purchase → localized testimonials matter
- Cultural context: Spanish business culture = WhatsApp + personal relationships > cold email
- Dialect: Spain Spanish ≠ Latin American Spanish (vocabulary, accent, business norms)

**Opportunity:** Build education marketplace with:
- Spanish-native instructors (not translations)
- Spain-specific examples (company sizes, labor laws, market context)
- WhatsApp as primary support channel
- Regional pricing (€/currency, not USD conversion)
- Local payment methods (Bizum, Tarjeta, SEPA)

**Players winning here:** Systeme.io (0% commission, Spanish UI), Hotmart (user base), but neither optimized for Spain.

---

### Gap #5: Community + Peer Learning

**The Problem:** Hotmart is course platform only; no community for students/creators
- Isolation = lower completion rates (10–15% for self-paced courses)
- No peer support, cohort-based learning, or creator networking

**Opportunity:** Add to infobiz platform:
- Discord community (per course + per topic)
- Cohort-based learning (live weekly sessions, peer accountability)
- Creator networking (forums, mastermind matching, affiliate co-promotion)
- Gamification (badges, leaderboards, peer rewards)

**Case Study:** Kajabi, Thrivecart add community features = 20%+ higher retention, better word-of-mouth.

---

### Gap #6: Regulatory Compliance (VeriFactu, RGPD, SEPE)

**The Problem:** Spanish regulations require:
- VeriFactu (Jan 2027 deadline): immediate digital reporting of training (SII system)
- RGPD: stricter than GDPR, fines up to 4% revenue
- SEPE/FUNDAE: training records, certificates, attendance tracking must be officially compliant

**Current Reality:** Hotmart, Teachable, Kajabi = minimal Spanish compliance; no VeriFactu module

**Opportunity:** Build compliance-first infobiz platform:
- Auto-generate VeriFactu reports (Jan 2027 deadline)
- RGPD data handling (DPA with providers, auto-deletion, DPIA templates)
- SEPE certificate templates (officially recognized formats)
- Audit trail (for labor inspections)

**Moat:** Regulatory compliance = high switching cost, defensible moat vs. global platforms.
