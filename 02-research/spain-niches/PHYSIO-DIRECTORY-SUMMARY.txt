KEY FINDINGS:
=============

1. MARKET SIZE
   - 62,000 licensed physiotherapists in Spain (80% = 49,600 in private sector)
   - Population of 47.5M → estimated 2.4M annual sessions in private sector
   - TAM: €96–156M (based on €40–65 per session)
   - Growing demand: home visits +25%/year, elderly care segment

2. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
   - Doctoralia: 30–40% category capture, but physiotherapy = 2–3% of base (underserved)
   - Top Doctors: 316 physiotherapists, 95% B2B SaaS revenue (€23.9M in 2023), premium pricing
   - Niche players (Fisioterapeutas.one, Tufisio, Fisioterapia.io): Static catalogs, NO booking integration
   - FisioNow: Madrid-only, home visits only, 30–40% commission (unsustainable for providers)
   - OPPORTUNITY: No specialized "Zocdoc for PT" platform exists in Spain

3. INSURANCE PARTNERSHIP POTENTIAL
   - ADESLAS (SegurCaixa): 58,000+ network professionals, referral payments €15–30 per referral
   - Sanitas: Extensive network, physician-prescribed rehabrehabilitation
   - CRITICAL ISSUE: No API for third-party integration found (2026)
   - Path: Direct B2B outreach required (6–12 month sales cycle)

4. MONETIZATION MODELS EVALUATED

   Model A (Commission-based, like Doctoralia):
   - Subscription: €15/mo, 12% commission on first session
   - ARPU for platform: €37/therapist/month
   - LTV: €56,400 (too high, unsustainable churn)
   - VERDICT: Weak, therapists resist commissions

   Model B (Subscription + Referrals) ✓ RECOMMENDED
   - Base: €35/mo, Premium: €65/mo (avg €53)
   - Referral: €3–5 per ADESLAS referral
   - Y1 ARR: €3.54M (5K subscribers)
   - Payback: 8–15 months, CAC €40–100
   - LTV at 2% churn: €775 (payback 2 months)
   - VERDICT: Strong, aligns with insurance incentives

   Model C (Hybrid subscription + commission):
   - Base: €25/mo + 8% commission on first 5 bookings
   - Y1 ARR: €3.08M (5K subscribers)
   - VERDICT: Balanced, but complex messaging

5. PATIENT BEHAVIOR
   - Search channels: Google 30%, referrals 40%, directories 20%, clinic direct 10%
   - Willingness to pay: €40–80 private, €15–30 copay on insurance
   - Avg treatment duration: 8–18 sessions (acute) or 52+ sessions (chronic)
   - LTV per patient: €320–1,200/year (if retained)
   - Growing interest in online consultations (pre-assessment, follow-up)

6. UNIT ECONOMICS (Model B - RECOMMENDED)
   - CAC (Facebook): €80–100 per therapist
   - Payback period: 2–3 months (at 2% churn)
   - LTV: €775 (conservative at 2% churn)
   - Y1 MRR: €147.5K (€132.5K subscription + €15K referrals)
   - Burn rate: €30K/month (M1-M6), ROI positive M9–M10
   - Path to €1M ARR: ~18 months at growth rate assumptions

7. GO/NO-GO ASSESSMENT

   GO SIGNALS ✅:
   - TAM €80M+, growing faster than GDP
   - 1,000 new physiotherapists enter market annually
   - ADESLAS/Sanitas actively seeking networks (partnership ready)
   - Niche gap: Doctoralia weak, Top Doctors expensive
   - Unit economics viable at 2–3% churn

   NO SIGNALS 🔴:
   - Doctoralia likely to copy (70% probability) — must move fast
   - Low therapist adoption of SaaS historically (need success stories)
   - ADESLAS/Sanitas integration slow (6–12 months)
   - New regulation VeriFactu (Jan 2027) — minimal impact

   RECOMMENDATION: CONDITIONAL GO
   Strategy: Start B2C (therapists + patients), skip ADESLAS in MVP.
   If 100+ paying subscribers in 12 weeks → invest in insurance integration.

9. KEY RISKS & MITIGATION

   Risk 1: Doctoralia copies + wins (70% probability)
   - Mitigation: Speed (launch in 16 weeks), sticky features (ADESLAS integration, AI recommendations), sell-to-Doctoralia exit at €5–10M if we capture 5% market

   Risk 2: Low physiotherapist churn (30% probability)
   - Mitigation: ROI dashboard, free first month, success team support, money-back guarantee if <5 new patients/month

   Risk 3: ADESLAS refuses integration (40% probability)
   - Mitigation: Start B2C only, approach miniature insurances, corporate wellness, physician referral networks as alternatives

   Risk 4: Regulation/compliance (20% probability)
   - Mitigation: Legal review for RGPD/VeriFactu, no medical licensing required for marketplace
