## NICHE 1: TOURIST RENTAL VUD COMPLIANCE SAAS

### Key Strengths
- ✓ Zero competitors (white space)
- ✓ €52–82M TAM (30x larger than VeriFactu)
- ✓ 330K addressable properties
- ✓ Low churn (3–5% monthly, non-discretionary spend)
- ✓ Strong regulatory urgency (€60K fine, Airbnb delisting)
- ✓ Fast payback (3–4 months)
- ✓ Healthy unit economics (LTV:CAC 1:7.2)
- ✓ Annual renewal revenue (not one-time)

### Key Weaknesses
- ✗ Regional API complexity (17 different systems)
- ✗ Low platform switching cost (hosts may choose competitors if built)
- ✗ Regulatory change risk (VUD could be modified/cancelled)
- ✗ Airbnb/Booking could build in-house (platform threat)
- ✗ Higher CAC (€65 blended)
- ✗ Host acquisition education cost (low awareness of solution)

### Critical Success Factors
1. **Move fast:** Capture 500–1000 customers by Q4 2026 before platform competition
2. **Build moats:** Multi-regional database, host community, integrations
3. **Risk mitigation:** E&O insurance, clear T&Cs, security certifications
4. **Diversify:** Bundle tax prep + insurance to reduce VUD dependency

### Realistic Year 1 Revenue
- Q1: €600–800 ARR (50–80 customers)
- Q2: €2.5–3.5K ARR (200–280 customers)
- Q3: €5–7K ARR (400–560 customers)
- Q4: €8–12K ARR (640–960 customers)
- **Year 1 Total:** €16–23K ARR (€1.3–1.9K MRR by EOY)

**Note:** Conservative (focused on survival, not hype). With aggressive GTM = 2–3x these numbers.

## NICHE 2: VERIFACTU SAAS — EXPAT AUTÓNOMOS

### Key Strengths
- ✓ Massive language gap (zero English-language invoicing for Spain)
- ✓ High-intent audience (legal requirement, fines up to €50K)
- ✓ Lower CAC (€50 blended, digital-native audience)
- ✓ Better gross margin (90% vs 85%)
- ✓ Smaller TAM = less competition likely
- ✓ Niche audience = defensible brand ("expat invoicing")
- ✓ VeriFactu Jan 2027 deadline = time window

### Key Weaknesses
- ✗ TAM 20–30x smaller (€1.7–3.2M vs €52–82M)
- ✗ High churn (5–7% monthly due to expat transience)
- ✗ Tax calculation liability (CRITICAL RISK)
- ✗ Requires tax expert on staff (operating cost)
- ✗ Existing Spanish competitors (Billin, Holded, Quipu)
- ✗ Holded adding EN version (2027) will fragment market
- ✗ Complex regulatory compliance (multiple tax treaties)

### Critical Success Factors
1. **Tax expertise:** Hire Spanish tax lawyer/accountant from day 1
2. **Error-free calculations:** Build test suite for 20+ scenarios (high liability)
3. **Churn mitigation:** Focus GTM on permanent-residency expats (lower churn)
4. **Documentation:** Archive/export features to support returning customers
5. **Insurance:** E&O policy covering tax calculation errors

### Realistic Year 1 Revenue
- Q1: €20–30 ARR (15–25 customers)
- Q2: €150–250 ARR (100–150 customers)
- Q3: €400–600 ARR (250–350 customers)
- Q4: €600–900 ARR (350–450 customers)
- **Year 1 Total:** €1.2–1.8K ARR (€100–150 MRR by EOY)

**Note:** Much slower ramp than VUD due to smaller TAM + higher churn + GTM friction.
