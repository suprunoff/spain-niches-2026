## 1. TAM SAM SOM Framework (Market Sizing)

**Source**: [Antler](https://www.antler.co/blog/tam-sam-som), [Going VC](https://www.goingvc.com/post/how-investors-use-tam-sam-som-to-evaluate-startups), [HubSpot](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/tam-sam-som)

**What It Is**: Hierarchical market sizing framework used by VCs and startups to estimate addressable market.

**The Three Metrics**:
1. **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** — Total revenue opportunity if you had 100% market share
2. **SAM (Serviceable Available Market)** — Segment you can realistically target (geography, pricing, product constraints)
3. **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)** — Realistic capture given competition and execution ability

**Calculation Approaches**:
- **Top-Down**: Start from broad industry figures, narrow down (demonstrates vision for investors)
- **Bottom-Up**: Build from specific customer segment + pricing data (shows credible path to revenue)
- **Cross-validate**: Use both approaches to verify estimates

**Implementation Steps**:
1. Define target customer segment clearly
2. Research total industry size (industry reports, government data)
3. Calculate SAM by removing geographic/product constraints
4. Estimate SOM using competition analysis + realistic market share expectations
5. Show year-1, year-3, year-5 projections

**Tools**: [PM Toolkit TAM SAM SOM Calculator](https://pmtoolkit.ai/calculators/market-sizing)

**Why It Matters**: VCs use TAM/SAM/SOM to evaluate startup potential. Successful startups (Uber, Airbnb, Slack) used this for both internal strategy and fundraising.

## 2. Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) Framework

**Sources**: [Strategyn](https://strategyn.com/jobs-to-be-done/), [Built In](https://builtin.com/articles/jobs-to-be-done-framework), [Christensen Institute](https://www.christenseninstitute.org/theory/jobs-to-be-done/)

**What It Is**: Customer-centric framework that defines markets around the specific "job" customers are trying to accomplish, not around products or demographics.

**Core Premise**:
- Customers don't buy products; they "hire" products to perform specific jobs
- The same job can be done by different products (e.g., a milkshake competes with breakfast foods, not just smoothies)

**Application to Niche Markets**:
- Identify underserved jobs → build niche solution → quickly own that market
- Redefine market segments by job rather than demographics → more targeted marketing
- Find hidden opportunities in unconventional market spaces

**Key Steps**:
1. Define your market around the job-to-be-done (not the product)
2. Interview target customers about what job they're trying to accomplish
3. Understand the context, obstacles, and alternatives they use
4. Design product/service around doing that job exceptionally well
5. Find niches where existing solutions do that job poorly

**Example Application**: Instead of selling "project management software," identify jobs like "help distributed teams stay synchronized" or "reduce context-switching overhead."

**Advantage**: Reveals overlooked customer segments and underserved niches that competitors miss.

## 3. Opportunity Scorecard (Opportunity Scoring Framework)

**Sources**: [Business Initiative](https://www.businessinitiative.org/business-tips/opportunity-scorecard-ranking-ideas-market-size-fit-competition/), [Driver Research](https://www.driveresearch.com/market-research-company-blog/opportunity-scoring/), [Jungle Scout](https://support.junglescout.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038189214-Opportunity-Score-vs-Niche-Score)

**What It Is**: Systematic scoring system to rank and prioritize niche opportunities.

**Core Logic**:
- High Importance + Low Satisfaction = **HIGH OPPORTUNITY** (customers want it but no one delivers it well)
- Low Importance + High Satisfaction = **LOW OPPORTUNITY** (nice to have, already solved well)

**Seven Critical Dimensions for Scoring**:
1. Personal fulfillment (do YOU care about this niche?)
2. Market demand (search volume, audience size)
3. Competition levels (saturated vs. underserved)
4. Monetization potential (affiliate, SaaS, digital products, services)
5. Audience size (large enough to sustain business?)
6. Evergreen relevance (trending vs. permanent need)
7. Personal alignment (skill fit, expertise)

**Scoring System**:
- **Niche Score**: Proprietary algorithm grading demand + competition + listing quality
- Score > 6 = promising opportunity
- Score < 3 = likely unprofitable or oversaturated

**Implementation**:
1. List candidate niches
2. Rate each on 1-10 for all 7 dimensions
3. Weight dimensions by importance (e.g., competition x2, demand x2)
4. Calculate total score
5. Rank and prioritize top candidates

**Viability Rule**: Niche must be small enough to avoid massive competition, but large enough (or growing) to sustain revenue.

## 4. Porter's Five Forces Analysis

**Sources**: [Harvard Business School](https://www.isc.hbs.edu/strategy/business-strategy/Pages/the-five-forces.aspx), [Cascade](https://www.cascade.app/blog/porters-5-forces)

**What It Is**: Classic competitive intensity framework analyzing structural attractiveness of a market.

**The Five Forces**:
1. **Threat of New Entrants** — How easy is it for competitors to enter?
2. **Threat of Substitutes** — What alternative solutions exist?
3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers** — How much control do customers have?
4. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** — How much control do suppliers have?
5. **Rivalry Among Competitors** — How intense is existing competition?

**Application to Niche Markets**:
- **High barriers to entry** = good (protects your niche)
- **Few substitutes** = good (less competition)
- **Low buyer power** = good (customers have fewer alternatives)
- **High supplier power** = bad (limits your margins)
- **Low rivalry** = good (less competition)

**For Online Niches Specifically**:
- E-commerce niches usually have LOW barriers to entry (dangerous)
- Focus strategy: Serve niche better than competitors → reduce rivalry through specialization
- Niche strategy works best when: specialized suppliers exist, few substitutes, strong buyer loyalty possible

**Limitations**: Works best for mature, well-established industries. May not capture emerging niches or fast-changing markets accurately.

**Implementation**:
1. Rate each of 5 forces on 1-10 scale
2. Calculate average (lower = more attractive)
3. Compare across candidate niches
4. Choose niches with favorable force combinations

## 5. Blue Ocean Strategy

**Sources**: [Arounda](https://arounda.agency/blog/top-10-blue-ocean-strategy-examples), [Blue Ocean Thinking](https://blueoceanthinking.substack.com/p/what-isnt-blue-ocean-strategy-niche), [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/resources/blue-ocean-strategy/)

**What It Is**: Create NEW market space instead of competing in crowded (Red Ocean) markets.

**Key Distinction**: Blue Ocean ≠ Niche Market
- **Niche**: Small segment of existing market (focusing on a subset of as-is customers)
- **Blue Ocean**: Redefine market boundaries to create space where competition is irrelevant

**The ERRC Framework** (Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create):
1. **Eliminate** — Remove features competitors obsess over
2. **Reduce** — Lower factors below industry standard
3. **Raise** — Increase factors above industry standard
4. **Create** — Invent new value factors customers didn't know they wanted

**Real-World Examples**:
- **Spotify**: Free massive library + freemium model (created new value) vs. iTunes' pay-per-song
- **Airbnb**: Authenticity + affordability (created new segment) vs. hotel industry standardization
- **Duolingo**: Free, fun, game-like learning (eliminated expensive formal training, created fun)

**Finding Your Blue Ocean**:
1. Analyze competitor feature matrix
2. Read negative reviews → identify what customers complain about
3. Apply ERRC to create new value curve
4. Test with early customers
5. Define new market space on value + price axes

**Key Insight**: Competition becomes irrelevant when you create a truly different value proposition.

## 6. Gap Analysis Framework

**Sources**: [Shopify](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/blog/gap-analysis), [Smartsheet](https://www.smartsheet.com/gap-analysis-method-examples), [Creately](https://creately.com/blog/strategy-and-planning/gap-analysis-tools/)

**What It Is**: Identify gaps between current market offerings and customer needs/trends.

**Types of Gap Analysis**:
- **Content Gap**: Topics audience searches for but you don't cover
- **Competitor Gap**: Features/services competitors offer that you don't
- **Market Gap**: Customer needs competitors don't fully address
- **Capability Gap**: Skills/resources competitors have but you lack

**Methodologies**:

### Content Gap Analysis
1. List target audience search queries (Google Trends, SEMrush, Ahrefs)
2. Audit your content coverage for each topic
3. Compare against top 3 competitors' content
4. Identify missing topics (high search volume + no coverage)
5. Prioritize gaps by search volume × ease of content creation

### Market Gap Analysis
1. Document customer pain points (surveys, interviews, reviews)
2. List existing solutions + their limitations
3. Identify unmet needs (mentioned by customers, not addressed)
4. Assess addressable market size for that gap
5. Check if gap is profitable (willingness to pay)

### Supporting Frameworks**:
- **SWOT Analysis**: Internal strengths/weaknesses + external opportunities/threats
- **PESTEL**: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal factors
- **McKinsey 7S**: Strategy, Structure, Systems, Shared Values, Skills, Style, Staff alignment

**Implementation**:
1. Define "current state" (what exists now)
2. Define "desired state" (what customers need)
3. Measure the gap quantitatively (size, revenue, customer count)
4. Prioritize gaps by impact × difficulty
5. Create action plan to fill highest-priority gaps

## 7. Niche Scoring System (Demand + Competition + Profitability)

**Sources**: [AmzScout](https://amzscout.net/blog/low-competition-niches/), [Ranktracker](https://www.ranktracker.com/blog/niche-finder/), [NichePop](https://www.nichepop.app/)

**What It Is**: Data-driven scoring system to evaluate niche viability across multiple dimensions.

**Key Metrics**:

| Metric | Good Signal | Poor Signal |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Search Volume** | 500-5000/month | <100 or >50K (red flags for both) |
| **Monthly Revenue** | $1K-$10K potential | <$100 or approaching saturation |
| **CPC (Cost-Per-Click)** | >$1 | <$0.50 (low monetization) |
| **Competition Level** | 5-15 competitors | >50 (oversaturated) or <2 (unproven) |
| **Trend** | Rising or stable | Declining |
| **Monthly Sales** | >200 units | <50 units (weak demand) |

**Profitability Indicators**:
- High demand (search volume, audience size)
- Clear monetization pathways (affiliate, SaaS, digital products, courses)
- Manageable competition (you can realistically compete)
- Growing audience (check Google Trends, YouTube growth)

**Scoring Formula** (Example):
```
Score = (Demand × 2) + (Monetization × 2) + (Competition inverse × 1) + (Evergreen × 1) / 6
```

**Interpretation**:
- Score > 7 = Excellent opportunity
- Score 5-7 = Good opportunity (verify with interviews)
- Score 3-5 = Risky (test before investing)
- Score < 3 = Avoid (oversaturated or low demand)

**Tools**: Jungle Scout, Ranktracker, NichePop, Ahrefs

## 9. Indie Hackers Niche Finding Method

**Sources**: [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/finding-a-market-and-an-underserved-niche-44952e1542), [Indie Hackers - How to Identify Micro-Niche](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/how-to-identify-a-micro-niche-that-s-perfect-for-saas-founders-19b7fab8aa)

**What It Is**: Practical 5-step method used by successful indie hackers and bootstrapped founders.

**Step 1: Start with What You Know**
- What industry have you worked in?
- What problems did you face?
- Who do you know that runs a business?
- What frustrates you in tools you use daily?

**Step 2: Identify Underserved Niche**
- Find extremely small niche within growing market
- Look for gaps where existing solutions are:
  - Expensive
  - Outdated
  - Incomplete
  - Don't exist for specific use case

**Step 3: Build Audience**
- Join communities (Reddit, Quora, LinkedIn, Discord, Facebook groups)
- Participate without selling
- Build trust and visibility

**Step 4: Ask About Problems**
- Conduct 50-100 interviews with people in niche
- Ask open-ended questions about pain points
- Listen for problems mentioned repeatedly
- Validate willingness to pay

**Step 5: Build & Monetize**
- Create product/service solving identified problems
- Charge from day one (test monetization assumption)
- Iterate based on customer feedback

**Micro-Niche Identification Formula**:
1. Identify broad category (e.g., "SaaS")
2. Add geography constraint (e.g., "SaaS for UK agencies")
3. Add job role constraint (e.g., "SaaS for UK agencies' project managers")
4. Add use case constraint (e.g., "SaaS for UK agencies' remote project managers managing distributed teams")

**Key Metrics**:
- 50-100 customer interviews = good confidence
- 72% of successful niche businesses do quarterly market trend analysis
- If you can't find 20 potential customers easily → niche is too small

## 10. Hacker News Niche Validation Patterns

**Sources**: [HN - Startup idea validation](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=3167676), [HN - Market gaps](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=22143386), [HN - Validation tool](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44881440)

**What It Is**: Community-tested validation patterns from veteran hackers.

**Pattern 1: Parallelized Validation**
- Don't do validation steps sequentially
- Run customer interviews, market research, prototyping in parallel
- Test demand while building MVP (save time)

**Pattern 2: Bootstrap-Friendly Niche Criteria**
- Niche accessible to one person working part-time
- Not dependent on external funding
- Has clear willingness to pay (B2B preferred)
- Problem is acute enough to solve quickly

**Pattern 3: Real-Time Sentiment Analysis**
- Tool: Fetch discussions from HN, Reddit, Twitter
- Summarize sentiment (positive/negative/neutral)
- Assign "validation score" based on discussion volume + sentiment
- Score > 0.7 = strong validation signal

**Pattern 4: YouTube Niche Profitability**
- Tool: ApexVix scans 400K+ channels
- Returns CPM ranges for niche
- Estimates revenue potential before investing months
- Avoids "wasted 8 months on $2 CPM" scenario

**Validation Checklist**:
- [ ] Can you find 20+ potential customers in 1 hour?
- [ ] Do customer interviews reveal urgent pain points?
- [ ] Existing solutions have clear limitations?
- [ ] Problem generates discussion online (HN, Reddit, Discord)?
- [ ] You can build MVP in < 1 month?
- [ ] Clear path to first $1K MRR?

## 11. Trend Identification Methods (Indie Hackers)

**Sources**: [Indie Hackers - 5 Ways to Find Trends](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/5-ways-to-find-trends-for-your-niche-b5e88d2ebd)

**Method 1: Keyword Research Tools**
- Tools: Google Trends, SEMrush, Ahrefs
- Track: Search volume + trend graphs
- Identify: Rising keywords (early entry opportunity)
- Avoid: Declining trends (dead-end niches)

**Method 2: Community Monitoring**
- Platforms: Reddit, Quora, LinkedIn groups, Discord, Facebook groups
- Tactic: Monitor for repeated questions, complaints, feature requests
- Signal: High engagement on specific topics = market demand

**Method 3: Competitor Analysis**
- Monitor: Product launches, marketing campaigns, content updates
- Identify: Emerging trends competitors are capitalizing on
- Act: Either enter earlier or find gap they're missing

**Method 4: Statistical Analysis**
- Create survey for target audience
- Conduct: Factor analysis + cluster analysis
- Segment: Group people by psychometrics + unmet needs
- Result: Very specific micro-niches (high specificity = less competition)

**Method 5: Reverse Engineering**
- Find: Successful products in related niches
- Analyze: Why are they successful?
- Apply: Same approach to underserved adjacent niche

## 12. Implementation Workflow (Recommended)

**Complete Niche Analysis Process** (Actionable steps):

### Phase 1: Discovery (Week 1-2)
1. Use Indie Hackers method to identify 5-10 candidate niches
2. Run TAM/SAM/SOM estimates for each
3. Create opportunity scorecard ranking them
4. Pick top 3 for validation

### Phase 2: Validation (Week 3-4)
1. Conduct Porter's Five Forces analysis for each
2. Run gap analysis against competitors
3. Execute 20-30 customer interviews
4. Collect monetization signals (affiliate programs, SaaS potential, course viability)

### Phase 3: Scoring & Ranking (Week 5)
1. Score each niche using Opportunity Scorecard
2. Calculate niche score (demand + competition + profitability)
3. Rank by score
4. Validate scoring with your interview insights

### Phase 4: Final Decision (Week 6)
1. Deep dive: JTBD analysis for top niche (what job are customers hiring?)
2. Blue Ocean check: Can you differentiate significantly?
3. Build confidence: Identify 10+ specific customers you could sell to
4. Make decision: Pick niche with highest score + personal alignment

### Phase 5: Validation Before Building (Week 7)
1. Create landing page for product
2. Drive 100-200 visitors from target audience
3. Measure: Email signups / interest (>10% = good signal)
4. Get pre-commitments from 5-10 customers
5. Only then: Start building MVP

## Key Principles

1. **Parallelization**: Run validation steps simultaneously, not sequentially
2. **Quantification**: Use scoring systems, not gut feel
3. **Customer Evidence**: 50+ interviews > market reports
4. **Willingness to Pay**: Validate monetization, not just demand
5. **Focus on Underserved**: Niche success = solving an acute problem better than anyone
6. **Avoid Oversaturation**: Niche must be small enough to dominate, large enough to sustain
7. **AI Acceleration**: Use Claude/AI agents to 10x research speed
8. **Iterate**: First niche hypothesis likely wrong — interview, learn, pivot
